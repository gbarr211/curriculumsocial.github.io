Business Protection Strategy

Koh Samui, Thailand

Executive Summary

This document outlines the comprehensive business protection strategy for launching Curriculum Social in Koh Samui, Thailand. As an innovative educational technology platform connecting local learners and families, Curriculum Social represents significant intellectual property that requires robust protection while navigating the Thai business and regulatory environment.

Our protection strategy prioritizes four key areas:

  • Intellectual property protection (trademark, copyright, trade secrets)
  • Strategic business registration leveraging Thailand's BOI incentives
  • Comprehensive confidentiality agreements
  • Compliance with Thai and international data protection regulations

Intellectual Property Protection

Trademark Protection

Register "Curriculum Social" name and logo with the Department of Intellectual Property (DIP) in Thailand.

  • Class 41: Education services
  • Class 42: Software as a service
  • Valid for 10 years with renewal option

Copyright Protection

Automatic protection under Thai Copyright Act BE 2537 for platform content and code.

  • Voluntary registration with DIP
  • Digital timestamping of original content
  • Protection lasts 50 years after creator's death

Trade Secrets

Protection of proprietary methodologies and algorithms under Trade Secrets Act BE 2545.

  • Internal confidentiality procedures
  • Access controls for sensitive information
  • Employer-employee confidentiality clauses

IP Action Plan

1. Trademark Registration Process

Submit application to Thailand's Department of Intellectual Property with distinctive logo design and class specifications. Estimated cost: 1,000 THB per application plus legal fees (25,000-30,000 THB). Processing time: 12-18 months.

2. Copyright Documentation

Compile comprehensive documentation of platform development, implement digital timestamping, and apply for voluntary copyright registration with Thailand's DIP. Estimated cost: 100-300 THB per registration.

3. Trade Secret Protection

Establish internal security protocols, implement access control systems, and develop information classification system. Document all reasonable steps taken to maintain secrecy.

Business Registration with BOI Incentives

Thailand's Board of Investment (BOI) offers significant incentives for educational technology businesses, which can be leveraged to optimize both business protection and financial advantages.

Recommended Structure: Thai Limited Company with BOI Promotion

Requirement Details
Legal Structure Thai Limited Company (Co., Ltd.)
Shareholders Minimum 3 shareholders (nominees possible with BOI privileges)
Registered Capital 2 million THB minimum (~$60,000 USD) for employing foreigners
Ownership 100% foreign ownership possible with BOI promotion
BOI Category 5.8 E-learning content / Digital content / Software development

BOI Incentives for EdTech

Tax Incentives

  • 5-8 years corporate income tax exemption
  • Double deduction for transportation, electricity and water costs
  • Import duty exemption on machinery
  • Import duty exemption on raw materials used in production for export

Non-Tax Incentives

  • Permission for 100% foreign ownership
  • Land ownership permission
  • Visa and work permit facilitation for foreign experts
  • Permission to bring in foreign skilled workers and experts

Business Registration Process

1. BOI Application

Submit application to BOI for promotion certificate. Include business plan, financial projections, and technology details. Estimated cost: 30,000-45,000 THB. Processing time: 40-90 working days.

2. Company Registration

Register with Department of Business Development once BOI approval is received. Submit memorandum of association, articles of association, and shareholder details. Estimated cost: 5,000-7,500 THB in government fees plus 25,000-50,000 THB legal assistance.

3. Tax Registration

Register for corporate income tax, VAT (if applicable), and withholding tax with the Revenue Department within 60 days of operations.

4. BOI Operational Certification

After establishment, apply for operational certification from BOI to activate tax incentives. This requires demonstrating that operations match the approved business plan.

Confidentiality Agreements

A robust confidentiality framework is essential to protect the Curriculum Social concept, technology, and business model during the development and launch phases.

Investor NDAs

  • Required before sharing detailed business plan
  • Non-compete provisions (2-year duration)
  • Specific clauses prohibiting similar educational platforms
  • Governed by Thai law with arbitration clause

Educator Agreements

  • Confidentiality of teaching methodologies
  • Protection of curriculum materials
  • Non-solicitation of platform families
  • Content ownership clarification
  • Limited exclusivity provisions

Developer Agreements

  • IP assignment for all developed code
  • Confidentiality of technical architecture
  • Non-competition in educational platforms
  • Source code protection provisions
  • Security protocols compliance

Key NDA Components

All confidentiality agreements should include:

  • Clear definition of confidential information (business model, teaching methodologies, algorithms)
  • Obligations of receiving party (non-disclosure, security measures)
  • Permitted uses of confidential information
  • Duration of confidentiality obligations (minimum 3 years)
  • Remedies for breach (injunctive relief, liquidated damages)
  • Return or destruction of confidential information
  • Governing law and dispute resolution (Thai law with optional international arbitration)

Implementation Strategy

Create a tiered information disclosure system, with progressive revelation of business details as relationships develop and appropriate agreements are executed. Maintain signed NDAs in secure digital repository with access logs.

Legal Framework for Student Data Protection

Educational platforms must comply with stringent data protection requirements, especially when handling children's data. Curriculum Social will implement a comprehensive data protection strategy compliant with both Thai and international standards.

Thailand Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA)

Thailand's PDPA came into full effect in June 2022, establishing comprehensive data protection requirements similar to the EU's GDPR. As an educational platform handling sensitive student data, Curriculum Social must ensure strict compliance.

Key PDPA Requirements:

  • Legal basis for data collection
  • Purpose limitation
  • Data minimization
  • Explicit consent for children's data
  • Data subject rights (access, correction, deletion)
  • Data security measures
  • Data breach notification within 72 hours

Special Considerations for Educational Data:

  • Parental consent required for children under 15
  • Prohibition on profiling without explicit consent
  • Higher security standards for educational records
  • Limitations on third-party data sharing
  • Data minimization principles particularly important

International Compliance Considerations

EU GDPR Compliance

While operating in Thailand, Curriculum Social may have EU users (expatriates) requiring GDPR compliance:

  • Appointment of EU representative if needed
  • Data processing agreements with all vendors
  • Additional user rights implementation
  • Data transfer mechanisms for EU data

Cross-Border Data Transfer

As an international platform serving expatriate families:

  • Implement standard contractual clauses
  • Maintain records of all data transfers
  • Ensure server locations comply with Thai PDPA
  • Consider data localization for sensitive information
  • Geographically distributed backup strategy

Implementation Plan

Data Protection Impact Assessment

Conduct comprehensive DPIA to identify specific risks related to student data and platform functionality.

Data Protection Policy Development

Create platform-specific policies addressing data collection, processing, retention, and sharing practices.

Consent Mechanism Implementation

Design age-appropriate consent flows with parental verification for users under 15 years old.

Security Measures

Implement encryption, access controls, and security monitoring appropriate for educational data.

Data Subject Rights Procedures

Establish clear processes for handling access requests, corrections, and deletion requests.
