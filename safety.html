Our Commitment to Child Safety & Data Protection

At Curriculum Social, the safety, wellbeing, and privacy of children is our highest priority. This policy outlines our comprehensive approach to ensuring a secure learning environment both physically and digitally. Our practices exceed standard requirements and reflect our core values of trust, respect, and responsibility.

This document serves as both our commitment to families and our guidelines for all staff, teachers, and venue partners. All participants in the Curriculum Social community are required to adhere to these policies without exception.

Child Safety Protocols

Two-Adult Supervision Requirement

Core Policy:

All Curriculum Social activities involving children must be supervised by a minimum of two authorized adults at all times, without exception.

  • No Exceptions: No learning session, activity, or event will proceed without at least two authorized adults present.
  • Visibility: Activities must take place in open, visible spaces or rooms with windows/viewing access.
  • Composition: The two adults should ideally come from different families to ensure oversight balance.
  • Documentation: Attendance records must note the supervising adults for each session.

Rationale:

This policy protects both children and adults by ensuring transparency, providing multiple perspectives in case of incidents, and reducing opportunity for inappropriate behavior.

Background Check Procedures

Core Policy:

All teachers, parent-teachers, and regular venue hosts must complete a comprehensive background verification process before participating in Curriculum Social activities.

Required Verifications:

  • Criminal Background Check: International and local checks based on residency history.
  • Identity Verification: Government-issued ID verification.
  • Reference Checks: Minimum two professional or character references.
  • Education/Qualification Verification: For subject-specific instructors.

Renewal and Monitoring:

  • Background checks must be renewed annually.
  • Self-disclosure of any new incidents is required within 48 hours.
  • Random spot-checks may be conducted throughout the year.

Monitoring Practices

Core Policy:

Curriculum Social implements age-appropriate monitoring practices that balance safety with privacy and dignity.

Physical Space Monitoring:

  • Open Spaces: Learning activities should occur in open, visible areas whenever possible.
  • Designated Areas: Clear designation of supervised vs. unsupervised areas.
  • Parent Observation: Parents may observe activities with prior arrangement.
  • Privacy-Conscious Design: Monitoring that respects child dignity and appropriate privacy.

Digital Space Monitoring:

  • Platform Oversight: All digital interactions on Curriculum Social platforms are monitored for safety.
  • AI Safeguards: Automated monitoring for inappropriate content or interactions.
  • Communication Records: All official communications are properly logged and preserved.

Incident Reporting Protocol

Core Policy:

All safety concerns must be reported promptly through established channels, with clear procedures for response and resolution.

Reporting Process:

  1. Immediate Response: Address immediate safety concerns first.
  2. Documentation: Complete incident report within 24 hours.
  3. Notification: Alert designated safety coordinator.
  4. Investigation: Prompt, thorough inquiry with appropriate confidentiality.
  5. Resolution: Clear communication of outcomes to all affected parties.

Mandatory Reporting:

All staff and teachers are mandatory reporters of suspected child abuse or neglect, in accordance with local laws and regulations.

Reports can be submitted via:

  • Email: [email protected]
  • Phone: [Safety Hotline]
  • Platform: Safety Report function in member portal
  • In Person: To any Curriculum Social administrator

Appropriate Touch Guidelines

Core Policy:

Curriculum Social maintains clear guidelines regarding appropriate physical contact to ensure child comfort and safety while respecting the need for positive human interaction.

Appropriate Touch:

  • High-fives, handshakes, and fist bumps
  • Brief shoulder or upper back touches for encouragement
  • Necessary assistance with educational activities
  • Safety-related contact (e.g., helping a child who has fallen)
  • Age-appropriate assistance with personal care for younger children when requested

Inappropriate Touch:

  • Kissing or intimate contact of any kind
  • Extended hugs or embraces
  • Touch on thighs, buttocks, chest, or other private areas
  • Unwanted or forced physical contact
  • Touch that serves no educational or supportive purpose

Child Autonomy: Children always have the right to decline physical contact of any kind without question or consequence.

Data Protection Measures

Data Collection Limitations

Core Policy:

Curriculum Social employs a data minimization approach, collecting only information that is directly necessary for educational purposes and platform functionality.

Children's Data Guidelines:

  • Minimum Necessary: Only essential data for learning functionality is collected.
  • Age-Appropriate: Different data standards apply based on child age.
  • Anonymization: Data is anonymized whenever possible for analytical purposes.
  • No Third-Party Sharing: Children's data is never sold or shared with third parties for marketing.
  • Regular Audits: Data collection practices are reviewed quarterly to ensure adherence to minimization principles.

Data Categories Collected:

Data Type Purpose Retention Period
Basic profile information Account management, matching Duration of membership
Learning progress data Educational assessment Duration of membership + 1 year
Communication content Community interaction 2 years
Platform usage metrics Service improvement Anonymized after 1 year

Data Storage Security

Core Policy:

All data, especially children's information, is protected through comprehensive technical and organizational security measures that meet or exceed industry standards.

Technical Safeguards:

  • Encryption: End-to-end encryption for all sensitive data both in transit and at rest.
  • Access Controls: Role-based access controls with principle of least privilege.
  • Authentication: Multi-factor authentication for staff accessing sensitive data.
  • Backup Protocols: Regular, secure backups with encryption and integrity verification.
  • Security Monitoring: 24/7 monitoring of systems for unauthorized access attempts.

Organizational Controls:

  • Staff Training: Regular data protection training for all team members.
  • Third-Party Vetting: Rigorous security assessment of any service providers.
  • Incident Response: Documented procedures for potential data breaches.
  • Regular Audits: Independent security audits conducted annually.
  • Data Protection Officer: Designated responsibility for overseeing data protection compliance.

AI Usage Boundaries with Children

Core Policy:

Curriculum Social implements strict guidelines governing AI interactions with children, ensuring age-appropriate, transparent, and supervised use of technology.

AI Safety Protocols:

  • Age-Appropriate AI: Different AI interaction models for different age groups.
  • Content Filtering: All AI-generated content pre-screened for appropriateness.
  • Supervised Interaction: AI learning sessions monitored by teachers/parents.
  • Transparent Design: Children are always aware they are interacting with AI.
  • Limited Data Collection: Minimal data collected during AI interactions.

AI Boundaries by Age Group:

Age Group Permitted AI Interaction Required Oversight
0-5 years Pre-approved content only Direct parental/teacher supervision
6-9 years Guided learning experiences Adult presence required
10-12 years Limited interactive learning Periodic adult monitoring
13+ years Full educational features Available adult guidance

AI Principles:

Our AI implementation follows ethical principles of transparency, safety, privacy, and human oversight. AI systems are designed to complement, never replace, human teaching and interaction.

Parental Consent Requirements

Core Policy:

Curriculum Social obtains verifiable parental consent for all aspects of platform participation, with tiered consent for different activities and data uses.

Consent Framework:

  • Verifiable Consent: Multi-factor verification of parental identity.
  • Granular Options: Parents can consent to specific activities and data uses.
  • Renewal: Consent renewed annually and for any material changes.
  • Withdrawal: Simple process to withdraw consent at any time.
  • Age-Verification: Reasonable steps to confirm child's age.

Required Consent Areas:

  • Account Creation: Basic platform participation
  • Data Collection: Specific types of personal information
  • AI Interaction: Use of AI-powered learning tools
  • Media Usage: Photos/videos of child for platform purposes
  • In-Person Activities: Participation in physical events
  • Communication: Direct messaging with teachers/peers

Additional Safety Measures

Communication Guidelines

Core Policy:

All communications involving children must follow established channels, with appropriate transparency and monitoring.

Platform Communication:

  • Official Channels: All communication must use official Curriculum Social platforms.
  • Parental Visibility: Parents have access to all communications involving their children.
  • Group Settings: One-to-one messages between adults and children are prohibited outside parent-visible channels.
  • Content Standards: All communications must adhere to appropriate language and content standards.

External Communication:

  • Prohibition: Staff and teachers may not connect with students on personal social media.
  • Personal Contact: Personal phone numbers, email addresses, or other contact information should not be exchanged.
  • Reporting: Any communication outside approved channels should be reported.

Photo/Video Policies

Core Policy:

Curriculum Social maintains strict protocols governing the capture, storage, usage, and sharing of images and videos featuring children.

Consent Requirements:

  • Explicit Permission: Specific parental consent required for any photo/video capturing.
  • Usage Limitations: Clear specification of how media will be used and for what duration.
  • Revocation Right: Parents may withdraw permission at any time with prompt removal of content.

Media Guidelines:

  • Appropriate Content: Children must be appropriately dressed and engaged in suitable activities.
  • Group Focus: Focus on group activities rather than individual children when possible.
  • Storage Security: Media files stored with encryption and strict access controls.
  • No Personal Devices: Photos/videos taken only with designated Curriculum Social devices.
  • No Identification: Children's full names never associated with images in public materials.

Safety Training Requirements

Core Policy:

All staff, teachers, and regular venue hosts must complete comprehensive safety training before interacting with children on the platform.

Required Training Components:

  • Child Protection Fundamentals: Recognition of abuse signs and reporting procedures.
  • Digital Safety: Online protection practices and appropriate digital interaction.
  • Emergency Response: First aid basics and emergency protocols.
  • Policy Compliance: Detailed understanding of all Curriculum Social safety policies.
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Appropriate interaction across diverse cultural backgrounds.

Training Schedule:

  • Initial Training: Comprehensive onboarding before any child interaction.
  • Refresher Courses: Annual renewal of safety certification.
  • Update Training: Additional training when policies significantly change.
  • Documentation: All training completion recorded and verified.

Policy Enforcement

Core Policy:

Curriculum Social enforces safety policies consistently and transparently, with clear consequences for violations and a commitment to continuous improvement.

Enforcement Process:

  1. Monitoring: Regular policy compliance audits and supervision.
  2. Investigation: Prompt, thorough review of potential violations.
  3. Documentation: Clear recording of all incidents and responses.
  4. Response: Proportional action based on violation severity.
  5. Appeal: Fair process for contesting enforcement decisions.

Potential Consequences:

  • Additional Training: For minor or unintentional violations.
  • Supervision: Increased monitoring for specified period.
  • Restriction: Limited platform access or capabilities.
  • Suspension: Temporary removal from platform activities.
  • Termination: Permanent removal for serious violations.

This Child Safety & Data Protection Policy is reviewed quarterly and updated as needed to reflect emerging best practices, regulatory changes, and community feedback.

All members of the Curriculum Social community will be notified of significant policy changes, with summaries of updates provided and training refreshed as needed.
