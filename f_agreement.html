This Family Membership Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into on the date of signature below between:

Curriculum Social, a provider of educational services ("we," "our," or "Provider") and

The parent(s) or legal guardian(s) ("you" or "Member") identified at the end of this Agreement.

By signing this Agreement, you are entering into a legally binding contract. Please read it carefully before signing.

1. Service Description

Curriculum Social is a hybrid education platform that combines:

  1. AI-powered learning: Self-paced educational content adapted to your child's learning style and progress.
  2. Human teaching: Live instruction from qualified teachers and parent-teachers in specific subject areas.
  3. Community-based social learning: In-person activities, field trips, and collaborative projects with other member families.

Our platform uses a Montessori-inspired approach while aligning with global educational standards to provide a comprehensive, personalized learning experience for children.

2. Membership Tiers and Fees

Curriculum Social offers three membership tiers to accommodate different needs and preferences:

2.1 Basic Membership

  • Monthly Fee: $100-150 per child
  • Access to core AI learning platform
  • Limited in-person activities (2-4 per month)
  • Basic progress tracking

2.2 Standard Membership

  • Monthly Fee: $200-300 per child
  • Full AI learning platform access
  • Regular in-person activities (8-12 per month)
  • Comprehensive progress tracking and customization
  • Limited teacher 1:1 sessions

2.3 Premium Membership

  • Monthly Fee: $350-500 per child
  • Priority access to all platform features
  • Unlimited in-person activities
  • Regular 1:1 teacher sessions
  • Advanced customization and specialized modules

Each membership tier has access to additional services at supplementary costs:

  • Specialized module packages: $30-50 per module
  • Additional 1:1 teacher sessions: $25-40 per session

Actual fees within the above ranges will be specified in your Membership Confirmation addendum to this Agreement.

3. Payment Terms

3.1 Payment Schedule

Membership fees are billed monthly in advance. Payment is due on the 1st of each month or on the start date of your membership.

3.2 Payment Methods

We accept credit/debit cards and bank transfers. Automatic recurring payment is the default option but can be changed to manual payment upon request.

3.3 Late Payments

Payments received after the 5th of the month will incur a late fee of $25. Accounts more than 30 days past due may result in suspension of services until payment is made.

3.4 Parent-Teacher Credit Option

If you choose to participate as a parent-teacher, you may receive tuition credits as follows:

  • 100% tuition waiver for one child with 8 hours of teaching per week
  • Additional compensation of $15-20/hour for teaching beyond tuition offset
  • Credit application details will be specified in a separate Teacher Agreement

3.5 Prorated Fees and Refunds

If you join mid-month, fees will be prorated. No refunds are provided for unused portions of the month if you choose to terminate services before the end of a billing period.

4. Parent Responsibilities

4.1 Educational Support

Parents agree to:

  • Support their child's learning journey by ensuring regular engagement with the platform
  • Provide necessary supervision during AI-guided learning sessions appropriate to the child's age
  • Facilitate attendance at scheduled in-person activities
  • Communicate regularly with teachers regarding child's progress

4.2 Technology Requirements

Parents are responsible for providing:

  • Reliable internet connection for online components
  • Appropriate device(s) as recommended for the child's age (tablet, computer, etc.)
  • Basic learning supplies as communicated for specific activities

4.3 Community Participation

Parents agree to:

  • Adhere to community guidelines and code of conduct
  • Provide timely RSVPs for in-person activities
  • Notify organizers of any cancellations at least 24 hours in advance when possible
  • Participate in parent feedback sessions to improve the community

4.4 Information Accuracy

Parents must provide accurate information regarding:

  • Child's age, educational history, and learning needs
  • Emergency contact information
  • Any medical conditions or allergies that may affect participation
  • Any learning accommodations required

5. Child Data Protection and Privacy

5.1 Data Collection

We collect only the data necessary to provide educational services, including:

  • Basic demographic information (child's name, age, grade level)
  • Learning progress and activity
  • Interaction with educational content
  • Participation in community activities

5.2 Data Use

Collected data is used exclusively for:

  • Personalizing educational content
  • Tracking progress against educational standards
  • Improving platform services
  • Ensuring child safety

5.3 Data Protection

We implement strict data protection measures, including:

  • End-to-end encryption of sensitive information
  • Limited access to personal data based on staff role
  • Regular security audits and updates
  • Compliance with COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) and GDPR where applicable

5.4 AI Usage Boundaries

Our AI systems:

  • Use age-appropriate content filters
  • Are regularly audited for bias and appropriateness
  • Process data with privacy by design principles
  • Use closed models for younger children to limit external data exposure

5.5 Parental Access

Parents have the right to:

  • Review all data collected about their child
  • Request deletion of data (subject to educational record requirements)
  • Opt-out of specific data collection with understanding of service limitations
  • Receive regular data collection reports upon request

6. Release Waivers

6.1 General Release

By signing this Agreement, you acknowledge and agree that participation in educational activities, particularly in-person events, carries inherent risks. You voluntarily agree to assume these risks and release Curriculum Social, its employees, contractors, volunteers, and other member families from liability for injuries or damages occurring during participation in our activities, except in cases of gross negligence or intentional misconduct.

6.2 Transportation

You are responsible for arranging transportation to and from in-person activities. When community transportation is arranged for field trips, separate permission forms will be provided.

6.3 Media Release

You grant permission for Curriculum Social to take and use photographs or videos of your child for educational documentation and marketing purposes. You may opt out of this provision in writing while understanding that some group documentation may still include your child in the background.

6.4 Academic Results

While we strive to provide high-quality educational experiences, we cannot guarantee specific academic outcomes, as results depend on many factors including student engagement and individual learning differences.

7. Safety Protocols and Acknowledgment

7.1 Two-Adult Supervision Policy

All in-person activities will be supervised by at least two adults who have completed our screening process. This policy is non-negotiable and essential to our safety framework.

7.2 Background Checks

All teachers, parent-teachers, and regular adult volunteers undergo background checks before working with children. You acknowledge this requirement extends to you if you choose to become a parent-teacher or regular volunteer.

7.3 Venue Safety

All venues used for in-person activities undergo safety verification. If you host activities in your home as a venue partner, you agree to comply with our safety standards and verification process.

7.4 Emergency Procedures

You acknowledge that you have received and reviewed our emergency procedures document and understand the protocols for different emergency scenarios.

7.5 Incident Reporting

You agree to promptly report any safety concerns or incidents involving your child or others to the designated community safety coordinator.

8. Intellectual Property Rights

8.1 Platform Content

All content provided through the Curriculum Social platform, including but not limited to curriculum materials, videos, activities, and AI-generated content, is protected by copyright and owned by Curriculum Social or its licensors.

8.2 License to Use

During the term of your membership, you are granted a limited, non-exclusive license to access and use the platform content for personal educational purposes only. This content may not be reproduced, distributed, or shared outside your immediate family without written permission.

8.3 User-Generated Content

Any content created by you or your child during platform activities remains your intellectual property. However, you grant Curriculum Social a non-exclusive license to use, modify, and share this content within the platform for educational purposes.

8.4 Collaborative Projects

Content created collaboratively during community activities is jointly owned by the participants. All families agree to allow such content to be shared within the community and used for platform promotional purposes.

9. Term and Cancellation

9.1 Term

This Agreement begins on the date of signing and continues until cancelled by either party according to the terms below.

9.2 Cancellation by Member

You may cancel your membership by providing 30 days written notice. Cancellation becomes effective at the end of the next full billing cycle after notice is received.

9.3 Cancellation by Provider

Curriculum Social may cancel this Agreement with 30 days notice for any reason, or immediately in cases of:

  • Violation of community guidelines
  • Non-payment of fees after 45 days
  • Actions that endanger community safety
  • Repeated violation of this Agreement

9.4 Effect of Cancellation

Upon cancellation:

  • Access to the platform will cease at the end of the final billing period
  • You will receive an export of your child's educational progress data
  • Your child's data will be retained for the minimum period required by law, then anonymized or deleted
  • Any outstanding fees remain payable

10. Dispute Resolution

10.1 Good Faith Resolution

Both parties agree to attempt to resolve any disputes through good faith discussion before pursuing formal legal remedies.

10.2 Mediation

If direct discussion fails to resolve a dispute, both parties agree to participate in mediation with a mutually agreed-upon mediator before pursuing litigation. The cost of mediation will be shared equally unless otherwise agreed.

10.3 Limited Arbitration

For disputes involving amounts under $5,000 USD, both parties agree to binding arbitration as an alternative to court proceedings. This does not prevent either party from seeking injunctive relief in cases where immediate action is required to prevent harm.

10.4 Community Mediation

For disputes involving multiple community members, a community mediation process may be utilized according to procedures outlined in the Community Guidelines document.

11.2 Severability

11.3 Entire Agreement

This Agreement, along with referenced documents (Community Guidelines, Emergency Procedures, and Membership Confirmation), constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter herein.

11.4 Amendments

Curriculum Social may amend this Agreement with 30 days written notice. Continued use of services after the notice period constitutes acceptance of amendments.
